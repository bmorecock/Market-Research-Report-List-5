<p><p>The edible grade gelatin market features three primary types based on their hydrolysis methods. Acid hydrolysis involves treating collagen with acids, resulting in gelatin that is typically more transparent and has improved gelling properties. Alkaline hydrolysis utilizes alkaline substances, producing gelatin with different functional characteristics, often preferred for specific applications. Enzyme preparation utilizes specific enzymes to break down collagen, yielding gelatin with distinct features, like enhanced clarity and emulsification properties. These variations cater to diverse food, pharmaceutical, and cosmetic industries.</p></p>
