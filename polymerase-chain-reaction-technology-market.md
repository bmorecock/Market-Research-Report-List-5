<p><p>The Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) technology market is primarily segmented into Digital Polymerase Chain Reaction (dPCR) and Real-Time Polymerase Chain Reaction (qPCR). dPCR provides absolute quantification of nucleic acids by partitioning samples into numerous reactions, enhancing sensitivity and precision, particularly for rare mutations. In contrast, qPCR allows for real-time monitoring of the amplification process, utilizing fluorescent dyes for quantitative analysis. Both methods are instrumental in various applications, including clinical diagnostics, research, and forensic investigations, driving growth in the biotechnology sector.</p></p>
