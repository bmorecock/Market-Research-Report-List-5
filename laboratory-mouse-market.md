<p><p>The laboratory mouse market comprises several types, including inbred strains, transgenic, and knockout mice. Inbred strains are genetically uniform, useful for consistent research outcomes. Transgenic mice are genetically modified to study gene functions and disease mechanisms. Knockout mice have specific genes disrupted, allowing researchers to investigate the effects of gene loss on biology. Additionally, there are other mouse models, including outbred and hybrid strains, which offer diverse genetic backgrounds for various experimental needs and applications in biomedical research.</p></p>
