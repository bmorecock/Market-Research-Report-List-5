<p><ul><li>Gas-Filled Detectors</li><li>Scintillators Detectors</li><li>Solid-State Detectors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Radiation detection in the medical and healthcare market encompasses three main types of detectors: gas-filled detectors, scintillator detectors, and solid-state detectors. Gas-filled detectors use ionization of gas to measure radiation levels, commonly employed in radiology. Scintillator detectors convert incoming radiation into visible light, facilitating high-resolution imaging, often used in nuclear medicine. Solid-state detectors utilize semiconductor materials to directly convert radiation into electrical signals, providing enhanced sensitivity and real-time data, making them ideal for advanced diagnostic applications.</p></p>
