<p><p>The air quality electrostatic precipitators (ESP) market is primarily categorized into wet and dry types. Wet ESPs use a liquid medium to enhance particle removal efficiency, making them effective for larger particles and sticky materials, often employed in industries with high moisture. Dry ESPs, on the other hand, utilize electric fields to capture dust and pollutants without moisture, suitable for facilities needing lower maintenance and energy costs. Both types play a crucial role in ensuring compliance with air quality standards.</p></p>
