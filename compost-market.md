<p><p>Compost market types include a variety of organic materials. Yard trimmings consist of grass clippings and branches, providing essential carbon and nutrients. Food wastes, often from households and restaurants, enrich compost with nitrogen. Leaves add structure and aeration. Manure from cows, horses, sheep, and poultry enhances nutrient content, each varying in richness. Mushroom compost is a byproduct of mushroom farming, offering additional organic matter. Vermicomposting involves using worms to decompose organic waste, resulting in nutrient-rich vermicompost for gardens and farms.</p></p>
