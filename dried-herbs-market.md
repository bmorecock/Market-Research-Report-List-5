<p><p>The dried herbs market consists primarily of two types: whole herbs and powdered herbs. Whole herbs maintain their structural integrity, offering stronger flavors and longer shelf life, making them popular for gourmet cooking and herbal remedies. Powdered herbs, on the other hand, are ground into fine particles, providing convenience and faster flavor release in recipes. Both types cater to varying consumer preferences, with whole herbs often used for aesthetic presentation in dishes, while powdered herbs are favored for their ease of use in seasoning and blends.</p></p>
