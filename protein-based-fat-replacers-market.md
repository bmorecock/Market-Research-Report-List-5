<p><p>Protein-based fat replacers are utilized in food products to mimic the mouthfeel and texture of fat while reducing the overall fat content. Two common types are microparticulated protein and modified whey protein concentrate. Microparticulated protein is created by breaking down protein molecules into smaller particles, which can thicken and stabilize products like dressings and sauces. On the other hand, modified whey protein concentrate is produced by treating whey protein to enhance its emulsifying properties, making it suitable for use in a wide range of products including baked goods and spreads.</p></p>
