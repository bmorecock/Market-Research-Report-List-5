<p><p>The saccharin market is categorized into two main types: insoluble saccharin and soluble saccharin. Insoluble saccharin is primarily used in applications where a sturdy, stable sweetening agent is needed, often in solid food products. In contrast, soluble saccharin dissolves easily in liquids, making it ideal for beverages and other fluid applications. Both forms cater to diverse industries, including food and beverage, pharmaceuticals, and personal care, driven by the increasing demand for low-calorie sweeteners and flavor enhancement.</p></p>
