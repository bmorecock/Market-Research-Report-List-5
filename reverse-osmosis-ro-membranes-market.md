<p><p>The Reverse Osmosis (RO) membranes market comprises mainly two types: cellulose-based films and thin film composite films. Cellulose-based films are derived from natural materials, offering good filtration but are less durable and have lower performance efficiency compared to their synthetic counterparts. Thin film composite films, on the other hand, incorporate advanced polymers, resulting in higher rejection rates, better fouling resistance, and enhanced longevity. This distinction highlights the balance between environmental sustainability and performance in water purification applications.</p></p>
