<p><p>High Density Interconnect (HDI) PCBs are advanced printed circuit boards characterized by a higher wiring density per unit area compared to conventional PCBs. They feature finer lines and spaces, smaller pad sizes, and multiple layers, which allow for more complex circuitry in a compact form factor. HDI PCBs are increasingly utilized in various sectors, including consumer electronics, telecommunications, automotive, and medical devices due to their enhanced performance, reduced weight, and ability to support miniaturization.</p><p>The HDI PCB market is witnessing substantial growth, driven by the surge in demand for miniaturized electronic devices and the rapid evolution of technology in industries such as smartphones and IoT devices. As electronic components become smaller and more powerful, manufacturers are gravitating towards HDI boards to meet these demands. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.9% during the forecast period. Key trends include the integration of advanced manufacturing techniques like laser drilling and advanced materials that enable even higher performance levels. Moreover, the increasing focus on environmental sustainability is prompting manufacturers to explore eco-friendly materials and processes, further shaping the future landscape of the HDI PCB market.</p></p>
