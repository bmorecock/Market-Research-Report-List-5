<p><p>The integral colorant market for concrete encompasses two main types: powder and liquid. Powder colorants are typically mixed directly into the concrete during the batching process, allowing for uniform color distribution and lasting effects. Liquid colorants, on the other hand, are added at various stages, including mixing or pouring, providing flexibility and ease of application. Both types enhance aesthetic appeal, with powder offering deep, vibrant hues, while liquid options are ideal for achieving subtle shades and tones in decorative concrete projects.</p></p>
