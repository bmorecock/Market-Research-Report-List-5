<p><p>The micro-computed tomography (micro-CT) system market is divided into two main types: ex-vivo and in-vivo micro-CT scanning. Ex-vivo micro-CT scanning involves imaging samples outside of a living organism, allowing for detailed analysis of biological tissues, skeletal structures, and materials in a controlled environment. In-vivo micro-CT scanning, on the other hand, captures high-resolution images of living subjects, enabling real-time monitoring of anatomical changes and disease progression. Both types are crucial for research and clinical applications in various fields, including biology and medicine.</p></p>
