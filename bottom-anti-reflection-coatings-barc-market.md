<p><p>Bottom Anti-Reflection Coatings (BARC) are essential in photolithography, improving the resolution of patterns on substrates by reducing reflections during exposure. The market for BARC is primarily divided into two types: organic and inorganic. Organic BARC materials are typically polymers that offer flexibility and ease of application, ideal for multiple layers. In contrast, inorganic BARC materials, made from compounds like silicon dioxide, provide better thermal stability and consistency but can be more complex to process. Both types enhance imaging performance in semiconductor manufacturing.</p></p>
