<p><p>The small-scale LNG market consists of two primary types: liquefaction terminals and regasification terminals. Liquefaction terminals are facilities that convert natural gas into liquid form, allowing for easier storage and transportation. These terminals typically serve smaller consumers or remote markets. Regasification terminals, on the other hand, convert LNG back into gaseous form for distribution and use in various applications, including power generation and industrial processes. Together, they facilitate the efficient supply chain and utilization of LNG in diverse regions.</p></p>
