<p><ul><li>Polycarbonate</li><li>Acrylic</li><li>Glass-Clad Polycarbonate</li><li>Poly-Vinyl Butyral (PVB)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The bullet-resistant auto glass market comprises various materials designed for enhanced safety. Polycarbonate is lightweight and impact-resistant but less scratch-resistant. Acrylic offers good clarity and impact resistance but is softer than polycarbonate. Glass-clad polycarbonate combines glass with polycarbonate for superior protection and aesthetics. Poly-Vinyl Butyral (PVB) is a durable interlayer in laminated glass systems that provides excellent impact resistance and sound insulation. Each type caters to different safety, weight, and visual requirements in automotive applications.</p></p>
