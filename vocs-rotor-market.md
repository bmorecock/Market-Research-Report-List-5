<p><p>The VOCs rotor market comprises two primary types: zeolite and activated carbon. Zeolite rotors utilize natural or synthetic zeolites to adsorb volatile organic compounds from industrial emissions, leveraging their unique pore structure for effective gas separation. In contrast, activated carbon rotors utilize porous carbon materials, offering high adsorption capacity and a broad range of application flexibility. Both technologies are aimed at reducing VOC emissions, enhancing air quality, and complying with environmental regulations while optimizing energy efficiency in industrial processes.</p></p>
