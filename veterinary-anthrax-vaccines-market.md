<p><p>The veterinary anthrax vaccines market consists of primarily two types: cell-free protective antigen (PA) vaccines and live vaccines. Cell-free PA vaccines utilize purified protective antigens extracted from the anthrax bacterium to stimulate an immune response without the risk of disease transmission. In contrast, live vaccines contain attenuated strains of the bacterium, providing robust immunity but requiring careful handling due to potential virulence. Both types aim to protect livestock and prevent outbreaks in animals, thereby safeguarding public health.</p></p>
